# Remote & Hybrid Work Policy
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
**Applies To:** All eligible employees of the Company
**Company Size Context:** Mid-size technology organization (200–500 employees)

## 1. Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this Remote & Hybrid Work Policy is to define clear, consistent, and fair guidelines for employees who work remotely or in a hybrid arrangement. The Company recognizes that flexible work models can improve productivity, employee satisfaction, and talent retention while maintaining high standards of performance, collaboration, data security, and customer service.
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees whose roles are designated as eligible for remote or hybrid work. Contractors and interns may be covered under separate agreements unless explicitly stated otherwise.
Remote work is a **privilege, not an entitlement**, and may be modified or revoked based on business needs, performance, security considerations, or policy violations.

## 2. Definitions
**Remote Work:** Performing job duties from a location other than a Company office on a full-time basis.
**Hybrid Work:** A structured schedule where employees work partially from a Company office and partially from a remote location.
**Remote Location:** An employee’s primary residence or another approved location within the same country of employment.
**Core Hours:** Designated hours during which employees must be available for collaboration.

## 3. Eligibility Criteria
### 3.1 Role-Based Eligibility
Remote or hybrid eligibility depends on job function, team requirements, and operational impact. Examples:
**Typically Eligible Roles:** - Software Engineers, QA Engineers - Product Managers - UI/UX Designers - Data Analysts - Technical Support (Tier 2 and above)
**Typically Not Eligible or Limited Eligibility Roles:** - IT Infrastructure and Onsite Support - Facilities and Office Operations - Hardware Lab Engineers - Roles requiring daily physical presence or regulated access
Final eligibility determinations are made by the employee’s manager in consultation with HR.
### 3.2 Performance and Tenure Requirements
To qualify for remote or hybrid work, employees must: - Have completed at least **3–6 months** of continuous employment (unless waived by management) - Meet or exceed performance expectations - Demonstrate self-management, accountability, and reliable communication - Have no active disciplinary actions
### 3.3 Geographic Limitations
Employees must work remotely from locations approved by the Company. Working from a different country or region requires prior written approval due to tax, legal, and data protection implications.

## 4. Work Schedule and Core Hours
### 4.1 Standard Working Hours
Employees are expected to maintain their standard working hours as defined in their employment agreement (e.g., 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM local time).
### 4.2 Core Hours
All remote and hybrid employees must be available during **core collaboration hours**, typically:
**11:00 AM – 4:00 PM (local time)**
During core hours, employees must be reachable via Slack, email, or video conferencing unless they are in a meeting, on approved leave, or have notified their team in advance.
### 4.3 Flexibility
Outside core hours, employees may adjust their schedules with manager approval, provided: - Total working hours are met - Business needs and deadlines are not impacted - Team collaboration remains effective

## 5. Hybrid Work Model
The Company supports a structured hybrid work approach to balance flexibility and in-person collaboration.
### 5.1 Standard Hybrid Options
Departments may adopt one of the following models:
**3 Days Office / 2 Days Remote** (e.g., Mon–Wed office, Thu–Fri remote)
**2 Days Office / 3 Days Remote** (e.g., Tue–Wed office, Mon/Thu/Fri remote)
Specific in-office days may be fixed at the team level to ensure collaboration.
### 5.2 Office Attendance Expectations
Employees are expected to: - Attend mandatory in-person meetings, planning sessions, and team events - Be present in the office on designated days - Maintain professional conduct and availability while onsite
Failure to comply with hybrid schedules may result in revocation of remote privileges.

## 6. Communication Protocols
Effective communication is critical in a distributed work environment.
### 6.1 Primary Tools
**Slack:** Primary real-time communication tool
**Email:** Formal communication, external correspondence, documentation
**Video Conferencing (e.g., Zoom, Google Meet):** Meetings, reviews, and collaboration
### 6.2 Response Time Expectations
**Slack:** Within 1 business hour during core hours
**Email:** Within 1 business day
**Urgent Matters:** Phone call or Slack direct message
### 6.3 Meetings
Employees must attend scheduled meetings on time with video enabled unless otherwise stated
Calendar availability must be kept up to date
Meeting recordings and notes should be shared when applicable

## 7. Equipment and Technology Requirements
### 7.1 Company-Provided Equipment
The Company may provide: - Laptop (standardized OS and security configuration) - External monitor (role-dependent) - Keyboard and mouse - Headset or webcam
All Company equipment remains Company property and must be returned upon request or termination.
### 7.2 Employee Responsibilities
Employees are responsible for: - Reliable high-speed internet connection (minimum 50 Mbps recommended) - Backup connectivity options where feasible - Maintaining equipment in good condition - Promptly reporting loss, theft, or damage

## 8. Data Security and VPN Requirements
### 8.1 Security Standards
Employees must comply with all Company information security policies while working remotely.
Mandatory requirements include: - Use of Company-approved **VPN** when accessing internal systems - Multi-factor authentication (MFA) - Company-managed antivirus and endpoint protection
### 8.2 Data Handling
Confidential information must not be accessed in public or unsecured locations
Screens should be locked when unattended
Company data must not be stored on personal devices or personal cloud accounts
Violations of security requirements may result in disciplinary action, including termination.

## 9. Home Office Setup Guidelines
### 9.1 Workspace Requirements
Employees must maintain a dedicated workspace that: - Is quiet, safe, and free from frequent interruptions - Allows confidential conversations without being overheard - Meets basic ergonomic standards
### 9.2 Ergonomics and Safety
Employees are encouraged to: - Use an adjustable chair and desk - Position monitors at eye level - Take regular breaks to prevent strain
The Company may provide ergonomic guidance or assessments upon request.

## 10. Performance Management and Evaluation
### 10.1 Performance Expectations
Remote and hybrid employees are held to the same performance standards as onsite employees.
Evaluation will focus on: - Achievement of goals and KPIs - Quality and timeliness of work - Communication and collaboration - Reliability and accountability
### 10.2 Monitoring and Trust
The Company does not engage in intrusive monitoring. Performance is measured by outcomes, not online presence.
Managers may conduct: - Regular one-on-one meetings - Quarterly performance reviews - Project-based assessments

## 11. Expense Reimbursement
### 11.1 Eligible Expenses
Subject to prior approval and Company policy, reimbursable expenses may include: - One-time home office setup allowance (e.g., desk, chair) - External monitor or ergonomic accessories - Partial internet cost reimbursement (monthly cap may apply)
### 11.2 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following are generally not reimbursable: - Rent or mortgage - Utilities (electricity, water) - Personal furniture not used primarily for work
All reimbursements must follow the Company’s expense submission process.

## 12. Compliance and Policy Review
Failure to comply with this policy may result in corrective action. The Company reserves the right to amend, suspend, or terminate remote and hybrid work arrangements at any time based on business needs.
This policy will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary.
