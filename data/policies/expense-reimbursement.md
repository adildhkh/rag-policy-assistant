# Expense Reimbursement Policy
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
**Applies To:** All eligible employees
**Company Context:** Mid-size technology company (200â€“500 employees)

## 1. Purpose and Scope
This Expense Reimbursement Policy establishes clear guidelines for business-related expenses incurred by employees while performing their job duties. The Company aims to ensure that employees are reimbursed fairly and promptly while maintaining financial control, compliance, and transparency.
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees. Contractors and consultants are subject to the terms outlined in their respective agreements.

## 2. General Principles
Expenses must be **reasonable, necessary, and directly related to Company business**
Employees are expected to exercise good judgment and cost consciousness
All expenses must comply with this policy and applicable laws
Reimbursement is not guaranteed for expenses that violate policy or lack documentation

## 3. Categories of Reimbursable Expenses
### 3.1 Travel Expenses
Reimbursable travel expenses include: - Airfare (economy class) - Train or bus fares - Hotel accommodations - Ground transportation (taxi, ride-share, car rental)
**Spending Limits:** - Airfare: Economy class only; upgrades not reimbursable - Hotels: Up to **USD 180/night** (standard cities) - Ride-share/taxi: Actual cost with receipt - Car rental: Mid-size or compact vehicle only
**Example:**
An employee traveling from Lahore to Dubai may book an economy flight and a hotel costing USD 160/night for 3 nights. Both are reimbursable.

### 3.2 Meal Expenses
Meals are reimbursable when incurred during business travel or client meetings.
**Daily Meal Limits (Non-Per Diem):** - Breakfast: USD 15 - Lunch: USD 25 - Dinner: USD 40
Alcohol is reimbursable only when part of a client meal and must not exceed **USD 20 per occasion**.

### 3.3 Software and Subscriptions
Reimbursable software expenses include: - Productivity tools (e.g., project management, design software) - Developer tools and licenses - Cloud services required for work
**Spending Limits:** - Up to **USD 300/year** without pre-approval - Above USD 300 requires manager and IT approval
**Example:**
A UX designer purchasing a USD 240 annual design tool subscription is eligible for reimbursement without additional approval.

### 3.4 Equipment and Hardware
Reimbursable equipment includes: - Monitors, keyboards, mouse - Headsets, webcams - Ergonomic accessories
**Spending Limits:** - Up to **USD 500 per calendar year** per employee - Individual items above USD 250 require pre-approval

## 5. Pre-Approval Requirements
Pre-approval is required for: - International travel - Any single expense exceeding **USD 250** - Conferences, training, or events above **USD 500** - Extended hotel stays (over 5 nights)
Approvals must be obtained via the Company expense system or email confirmation before the expense is incurred.

## 6. Receipts and Documentation Requirements
Original itemized receipts are required for all expenses above **USD 25**
Receipts must clearly show date, vendor, amount, and currency
Digital receipts (PDF or image) are acceptable
Credit card statements alone are not sufficient
Missing receipts may result in delayed or denied reimbursement.

## 7. Per Diem Rates
Employees may opt for per diem reimbursement instead of itemized meals.
### 7.1 Standard Per Diem Rates
Per diem covers meals and incidental expenses only and does not require meal receipts.

## 8. Corporate Credit Card Policy
### 8.1 Issuance and Usage
Corporate cards are issued to employees with frequent travel or purchasing needs
Cards must be used solely for business expenses
Personal expenses charged to corporate cards must be repaid immediately
### 8.2 Responsibility
Cardholders are responsible for safeguarding the card
Lost or stolen cards must be reported within 24 hours
Misuse of corporate cards may result in disciplinary action.

## 9. Reimbursement Submission Process
### 9.1 Submission Timeline
Expense reports must be submitted within **30 days** of incurring the expense
Reports submitted after 60 days may be rejected
### 9.2 Submission Steps
Log into the Company expense management system
Enter expense details and categories
Upload receipts
Submit for approval

## 11. Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following expenses are not reimbursable: - Personal entertainment (movies, gym fees) - Luxury travel (business/first class flights) - Traffic fines or penalties - Personal grooming (haircuts, spa) - Family or companion travel costs
**Example:**
Hotel minibar snacks or in-room movies are considered personal expenses and will not be reimbursed.

## 12. Compliance and Policy Review
Failure to comply with this policy may result in denial of reimbursement and disciplinary action. The Company reserves the right to audit expense reports and amend this policy as business needs evolve.
