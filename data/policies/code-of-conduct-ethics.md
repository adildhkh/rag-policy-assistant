# Code of Conduct & Ethics Policy
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
**Applies To:** All employees, officers, contractors, and representatives
**Company Context:** Mid-size technology company (200â€“500 employees)

## 1. Purpose and Values
The Company is committed to maintaining a workplace founded on integrity, respect, professionalism, and ethical behavior. This Code of Conduct & Ethics Policy defines the standards of behavior expected from all individuals representing the Company.
Our core values include: - Integrity in all business dealings - Respect for individuals and diversity - Accountability for actions and decisions - Compliance with laws, regulations, and internal policies
This policy applies equally to remote, hybrid, and onsite work environments.

## 2. Expected Workplace Behavior and Professionalism
Employees are expected to: - Treat colleagues, clients, partners, and vendors with respect and courtesy - Communicate professionally, whether in person, email, chat tools, or video calls - Act honestly and responsibly in all work-related activities - Avoid disruptive, aggressive, or abusive behavior
**Examples:** - Acceptable: Constructive feedback delivered respectfully during a team meeting - Unacceptable: Publicly insulting a colleague in a Slack channel or meeting
Unprofessional behavior may result in corrective action regardless of intent.

## 3. Anti-Discrimination and Anti-Harassment Policy
### 3.1 Equal Opportunity
The Company is an equal opportunity employer and does not tolerate discrimination based on: - Race, color, ethnicity, or nationality - Gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation - Religion or belief - Age, disability, or medical condition - Marital or family status
Employment decisions are based on merit, qualifications, and business needs.
### 3.2 Harassment-Free Workplace
Harassment of any kind is strictly prohibited, including: - Verbal harassment (slurs, jokes, offensive comments) - Physical harassment (unwanted touching) - Visual harassment (inappropriate images or messages) - Sexual harassment (unwelcome advances, requests for favors)
**Example:**
Repeatedly sending suggestive messages to a coworker, even outside work hours, is considered harassment.

## 4. Conflict of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with Company interests.
### 4.1 Common Conflicts
Outside employment with a competitor
Financial interest in a vendor or client
Hiring or supervising close relatives without disclosure
### 4.2 Disclosure Requirement
All actual or potential conflicts must be disclosed to HR or Legal.
**Example:**
An employee whose spouse owns a company bidding for a Company contract must disclose this relationship and recuse themselves from the decision.

## 5. Gifts and Entertainment Policy
### 5.1 Acceptable Gifts
Nominal gifts of **USD 50 or less** may be accepted occasionally
Meals or entertainment of reasonable value directly related to business
### 5.2 Prohibited Gifts
Cash or cash equivalents
Gifts intended to influence business decisions
Lavish or frequent entertainment
**Example:**
Accepting a modest branded gift from a vendor is acceptable; accepting an expensive smartwatch is not.

## 6. Whistleblower Protection
The Company encourages employees to report suspected misconduct without fear of retaliation.
Reports may be made confidentially
Retaliation against whistleblowers is strictly prohibited
Any retaliation will result in disciplinary action
Good-faith reporting is protected even if allegations are not substantiated.

## 7. Social Media Conduct
Employees must exercise judgment when using social media.
Guidelines include: - Do not disclose confidential or internal information - Do not represent personal opinions as Company positions - Avoid harassing, discriminatory, or offensive content
**Example:**
Posting screenshots of internal emails or Slack discussions on social media is prohibited.

## 8. Dress Code and Appearance
Employees are expected to maintain a professional appearance appropriate to their role.
### 8.1 General Guidelines
Clean, neat, and appropriate attire
Business casual is standard unless otherwise specified
### 8.2 Flexibility
Remote employees should dress professionally for video calls.
**Example:**
Wearing casual attire is acceptable for remote work, but appearing in inappropriate clothing on client video calls is not.

## 9. Substance Abuse Policy
The Company maintains a drug- and alcohol-free workplace.
Use, possession, or distribution of illegal substances is prohibited
Reporting to work under the influence is prohibited
Limited alcohol consumption may be permitted at Company-sponsored events
Violations may result in immediate disciplinary action.

## 10. Workplace Relationships
Professional conduct must be maintained in all workplace relationships.
Romantic relationships between managers and direct reports are prohibited
Relationships must be disclosed where conflicts may arise
Favoritism or perceived favoritism is not allowed
**Example:**
A manager may not directly supervise a partner or spouse.

## 11. Reporting Violations
Employees are encouraged to report violations promptly.
### 11.1 Reporting Channels
Direct manager
Human Resources
Ethics or compliance hotline
Reports may be made anonymously where permitted by law.

## 12. Investigation Process
All reports will be handled confidentially
Investigations will be fair, timely, and impartial
Employees are expected to cooperate fully
Corrective actions may include counseling, warnings, suspension, or termination.

## 13. Compliance and Acknowledgment
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract.
This policy will be reviewed annually.
**Approved By:** Human Resources, Legal, and Executive Management
