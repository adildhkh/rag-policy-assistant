# Professional Development & Training Policy
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
**Applies To:** All eligible employees
**Company Context:** Mid-size technology company (200–500 employees)

## 1. Purpose and Philosophy
The Company is committed to continuous learning and professional growth as key drivers of innovation, performance, and employee engagement. This Professional Development & Training Policy outlines the resources, financial support, and time allowances available to help employees enhance their skills, advance their careers, and meet evolving business needs.
This policy applies to onsite, hybrid, and remote employees unless otherwise stated.

## 2. Annual Training Budget
### 2.1 Standard Training Allowance
Each full-time employee is eligible for an **annual professional development budget of USD 1,500** per calendar year.
The budget may be used for approved external training, certifications, conferences, and learning materials.
### 2.2 Role-Based Adjustments
Certain roles may qualify for higher budgets due to specialized skill requirements:
Unused training budgets do not carry over to the next year unless approved by management.
**Example:**
A backend engineer may use up to USD 2,500 in a year to attend a cloud architecture course and renew a certification.

## 3. Eligible Training Types
Approved training activities include:
Professional courses (online or in-person)
Industry conferences and seminars
Technical and professional certifications
Workshops and bootcamps
Job-related books and learning subscriptions
### 3.1 Non-Eligible Training
The following are generally not covered: - Training unrelated to current or future Company roles - Hobby or recreational courses - Repeated training without demonstrated benefit

## 4. Required vs. Optional Training
### 4.1 Required Training
Mandatory training may include: - Information security and data protection - Code of conduct and ethics - Health and safety - Role-specific compliance training
Required training costs are fully covered by the Company and **do not count** against the employee’s annual training budget.
### 4.2 Optional Training
Optional training supports skill development and career growth and is funded through the annual training budget.

## 5. Training Request and Approval Process
### 5.1 Request Submission
Employees must submit training requests through the Company HR or learning management system, including: - Training description and provider - Cost breakdown - Business justification - Time commitment
### 5.2 Approval Workflow
Approval is based on relevance, budget availability, and performance considerations.

## 6. Conference Attendance Guidelines
### 6.1 Eligibility
Employees may attend up to **one major conference per year**, subject to approval.
### 6.2 Covered Costs
Conference registration
Travel and accommodation (per Company Expense Policy)
Meals (per diem or actuals)
### 6.3 Knowledge Sharing
Employees attending conferences are expected to: - Share key learnings with their team - Provide a short summary or presentation
**Example:**
An employee attending a USD 900 technology conference must present key takeaways in a team meeting.

## 7. Tuition Reimbursement Program
### 7.1 Eligibility
Employees with at least **12 months of service** may apply for tuition reimbursement.
### 7.2 Covered Education
Accredited degree or diploma programs
Job-related certifications or extended courses
### 7.3 Reimbursement Limits
Up to **USD 3,000 per year**
Reimbursement provided after successful course completion (minimum grade or pass status)
Employees must remain with the Company for at least **12 months** after reimbursement or repay the amount on a prorated basis.

## 8. Internal Training and Mentorship Programs
### 8.1 Internal Training
The Company offers: - Technical lunch-and-learn sessions - Leadership and management workshops - Cross-functional training programs
### 8.2 Mentorship
Voluntary mentorship program pairing junior and senior employees
Mentorship goals defined at program start
Typical duration: 6 months

## 9. Skills Development Plans
Managers and employees are encouraged to create **annual Individual Development Plans (IDPs)** covering: - Current skill gaps - Target skills aligned with career goals - Planned training activities
IDPs are reviewed during performance cycles.

## 10. Certification Support and Maintenance
### 10.1 Certification Costs
The Company may cover: - Initial certification exam fees - Renewal or maintenance fees - Required continuing education credits
### 10.2 Certification Agreements
For high-cost certifications (above **USD 2,000**), a retention agreement may be required.

## 11. Time Off for Training and Exams
### 11.1 Training Time
Employees may use work hours for approved training
Long-duration courses may require partial PTO usage
### 11.2 Exam Leave
Up to **2 paid days per year** for certification exams
Additional time may be approved by the manager
**Example:**
An employee preparing for a professional certification exam may take one paid exam day and one PTO day.

## 12. Policy Review and Compliance
Failure to comply with this policy may result in denial of reimbursement. The Company reserves the right to modify this policy based on business needs.
