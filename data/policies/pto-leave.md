# Paid Time Off (PTO) & Leave Policy
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
**Applies To:** All eligible employees
**Company Context:** Mid-size technology company (200–500 employees)

## 1. Purpose and Philosophy
The Company is committed to supporting employee well-being, work–life balance, and long-term engagement. This Paid Time Off (PTO) & Leave Policy provides a structured, transparent framework for vacation, sick leave, parental leave, and other time-off provisions while ensuring continuity of business operations.
This policy applies equally to remote, hybrid, and onsite employees unless otherwise specified.

## 2. Eligibility
Full-time employees are eligible for all PTO and leave benefits outlined in this policy.
Part-time employees may be eligible for prorated benefits as defined in their employment agreement.
Contractors, interns, and consultants are excluded unless contractually stated.

## 3. Annual Vacation (PTO) Accrual
### 3.1 Accrual Method
Vacation PTO accrues monthly and is available for use after completion of the probationary period (typically 3 months).
### 3.2 Accrual by Tenure
**Example:**
An employee with 4 years of service accrues 1.5 days per month. By the end of June, they will have accrued 9 PTO days.

## 4. Sick Leave
### 4.1 Entitlement
Employees are entitled to **10 paid sick days per calendar year**, accrued monthly or granted upfront based on local law.
### 4.2 Usage Guidelines
Sick leave may be used for personal illness, injury, or medical appointments
Employees may also use sick leave to care for an immediate family member
For absences exceeding **3 consecutive working days**, a medical certificate may be required
### 4.3 Carryover
Unused sick leave may carry over up to **10 days**, but the annual usage cap remains unchanged.

## 5. Parental Leave
### 5.1 Maternity Leave
**16 weeks of paid maternity leave** for the primary caregiver
Available to employees with at least 12 months of service
May be taken continuously or as approved by HR
### 5.2 Paternity / Partner Leave
**4 weeks of paid leave** for non-primary caregivers
Must be taken within 6 months of the child’s birth or adoption
### 5.3 Adoption and Surrogacy
Parental leave benefits apply equally to adoption and surrogacy cases.
**Example:**
An employee adopting a child is entitled to the same maternity or partner leave benefits depending on caregiving role.

## 6. Bereavement Leave
Employees may take paid bereavement leave in the event of a death in the family.
Additional unpaid leave may be approved on a case-by-case basis.

## 7. Public Holidays
The Company observes the following public holidays (may vary by country):
New Year’s Day
Independence / National Day
Labor Day
Religious Holidays (e.g., Eid, Christmas – per regional calendar)
Company-designated holidays (1–2 floating days)
When a holiday falls on a weekend, it may be observed on the nearest weekday.

## 8. PTO Request and Approval Process
### 8.1 Request Procedure
PTO requests must be submitted through the Company HR system
Requests should be made at least **10 working days in advance** for planned leave
Emergency leave should be reported as soon as reasonably possible
### 8.2 Approval Criteria
Managers approve PTO based on: - Team coverage requirements - Project deadlines - Fair distribution of leave
Approval or rejection should be communicated within **3 business days**.

## 9. Maximum Carryover Limits
Carryover balances must be used within the first quarter of the following year unless otherwise approved.

## 10. PTO Payout on Termination
Upon termination of employment:
Accrued and unused **vacation PTO** will be paid out in accordance with local labor laws
Sick leave is not paid out unless legally required
PTO balances are calculated up to the employee’s final working day

## 11. Emergency Leave
Emergency leave may be granted for unforeseen circumstances such as: - Serious illness or hospitalization of an immediate family member - Natural disasters - Urgent legal obligations
Up to **5 paid emergency leave days per year** may be approved at management discretion.

## 12. Sabbatical Leave
### 12.1 Eligibility
Employees with **7+ years of continuous service** may apply for a sabbatical.
### 12.2 Structure
### 12.3 Conditions
Sabbaticals must be approved at least 6 months in advance
Role continuity and knowledge transfer plans are required
Employees must return for at least 12 months post-sabbatical

## 13. Policy Compliance and Review
This policy does not replace statutory leave entitlements and will be applied in compliance with applicable labor laws. The Company reserves the right to amend this policy at any time.
**Approved By:** Human Resources & Executive Management
