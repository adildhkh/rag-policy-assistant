# Data Privacy & GDPR Compliance Policy
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, customers, and third parties
**Company Context:** Mid-size technology company (200–500 employees)

## 1. Purpose and Scope
This Data Privacy & GDPR Compliance Policy outlines how the Company collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal data in compliance with the **EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and other applicable data protection laws.
The Company is committed to safeguarding personal data and respecting the privacy rights of individuals, including employees, customers, partners, and website visitors.
This policy applies to: - All personal data processed by the Company - All systems, applications, and processes handling personal data - All employees and third parties acting on behalf of the Company

## 2. Data Protection Principles
The Company processes personal data in accordance with the following GDPR principles:
**Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency** – Data is processed lawfully and transparently.
**Purpose Limitation** – Data is collected for specific, explicit purposes.
**Data Minimization** – Only data necessary for the stated purpose is collected.
**Accuracy** – Reasonable steps are taken to keep data accurate and up to date.
**Storage Limitation** – Data is retained only as long as necessary.
**Integrity and Confidentiality** – Data is protected against unauthorized access or loss.
**Accountability** – The Company is responsible for compliance and documentation.

## 3. Legal Basis for Processing Personal Data
The Company relies on one or more of the following legal bases:
**Consent:** Explicit consent for marketing communications or optional services
**Contractual Necessity:** Processing required to fulfill a contract (e.g., customer services, payroll)
**Legal Obligation:** Compliance with employment, tax, or regulatory requirements
**Legitimate Interests:** Business operations that do not override individual rights
**Example:**
Processing employee payroll data is necessary to fulfill contractual and legal obligations.

## 6. Data Retention Periods
Data is securely deleted or anonymized once retention periods expire.

## 7. Third-Party Data Sharing and Processors
The Company may share personal data with: - Payroll and benefits providers - Cloud hosting and SaaS vendors - Payment processors - Professional advisors (legal, audit)
All third parties must: - Sign Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) - Implement appropriate technical and organizational safeguards - Process data only on documented instructions

## 8. International Data Transfers
Where personal data is transferred outside the EU/EEA, the Company ensures appropriate safeguards, including:
EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
Adequacy decisions by the European Commission
Additional technical safeguards (encryption, access controls)
**Example:**
Data stored on cloud servers in the US is protected using SCCs and encryption at rest and in transit.

## 9. Data Breach Notification Procedures
A personal data breach includes unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure of personal data.
### 9.1 Internal Reporting
All suspected breaches must be reported to IT Security and the DPO **within 24 hours** of discovery
### 9.2 Assessment and Notification
The DPO will assess risk within **48 hours**
Supervisory authorities will be notified **within 72 hours** where required
Affected individuals will be notified without undue delay if there is high risk
All breaches are documented in the breach register.

## 10. Privacy by Design and by Default
Privacy considerations must be embedded into systems and processes from the outset.
Requirements include: - Data protection impact assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing - Default privacy-friendly system settings - Access controls and role-based permissions

## 11. Employee Training and Awareness
Mandatory GDPR and data privacy training upon onboarding
Annual refresher training for all employees
Specialized training for roles handling sensitive data
Failure to complete required training may result in disciplinary action.
